Garden Grow

A therapeutic hand-tracking gardening game for people living with dementia and their care partners

Game Overview

The elevator pitch, key differentiators, and what makes Garden Grow unique

🌱 Elevator Pitch

"An old garden has been waiting for someone special to help it bloom again..."


Garden Grow brings grandparents and grandchildren together through gentle, camera-powered gardening that adapts to every player. No controllers, no touchscreens — just hands and a webcam.

★ Key Differentiators

  • Camera-based hand tracking (no controllers)
  • Real-time Dynamic Difficulty Adjustment per player
  • Co-operative & competitive multiplayer on shared screen
  • Fully synthesised audio (no external files)
  • Runs in any modern browser — no install needed

💚 Core Values

  • No failure states — every interaction is rewarded
  • Gentle, intuitive hand controls
  • Social connection — intergenerational 2-player play
  • Therapeutic fine motor + sensory engagement
  • Adaptive difficulty meets each player where they are

Design Pillars

The four core experiences driving every design decision

🏃 Physical Activity

Exercise for the body

  • Natural hand movements: reaching, hovering, grasping
  • Maps directly to physiotherapy exercises
  • Supports both seated and standing play
  • Fine and gross motor engagement

🧠 Cognitive Stimulation

Exercise for the mind

  • Sequential task planning (plant → water → grow)
  • Monitoring multiple plant needs simultaneously
  • Pattern recognition across plant types
  • Gentle decision-making without time pressure

🤝 Social Engagement

Connection over correction

  • 2-player shared screen designed for intergenerational pairs
  • Co-op teamwork moments (Magic Pumpkin)
  • Competitive but always celebratory language
  • "Us vs. The Garden" — never adversarial

💜 Wellbeing Support

Emotional & psychological nurturing

  • Nostalgic garden themes and familiar activities
  • Narrative purpose gives each session meaning
  • No failure, only growth — every round celebrates
  • Gentle, calming synthesised audio

Target Audience

Designed for a grandmother with dementia and her teenage grandson, side by side

👵

Primary: Person Living with Dementia

Core User

  • Benefits from sensory stimulation & repetitive positive actions
  • May not understand complex instructions — game must be self-evident
👦

Secondary: Care Partner

Play Partner
  • Teenager, adult child, or professional caregiver
  • Plays alongside in 2-player mode
  • Provides encouragement and shared experience
  • Benefits from structured, positive activity together
🧒

Tertiary: Clinical Staff

Facilitator
  • Nurses, caregivers, activity coordinators
  • Variable technology proficiency
  • Limited time per resident
  • Need setup under 30 seconds

Gameplay Mechanics

Hand tracking, player assignment, and interaction design

✋ Hand Tracking

MediaPipe Hands (legacy @mediapipe/hands via CDN)

  • Max 2 hands tracked (1 per player in 2P mode)
  • 6 collision points per hand: palm center + 5 fingertips
  • Camera resolution: 640×480
  • Model complexity: 0 (fastest)
  • Confidence: 0.5 detection, 0.3 tracking

👀 Player Assignment

Hands sorted by wrist x-position in camera frame:

  • Leftmost in camera → Player 1 (right side of mirrored screen)
  • Rightmost in camera → Player 2 (left side of mirrored screen)
  • Canvas mirrored with CSS scaleX(-1)
  • MediaPipe "Left" hand = user's actual RIGHT hand

🧶 Cartoon Gloves

ModePlayerOutlineFill
SoloSingle TealWhite
2-PlayerPlayer 1 OrangeWarm White
2-PlayerPlayer 2 BlueCool White

Puffy fingertips with highlight shines, colored outer glow, 80% of raw hand size.

🔄 Calibration Flow

1. Welcome → "Start Growing" (3s hand hover) 2. Player Select → "1 Player" or "2 Players" 3. Mode Select (if 2P) → Co-op or Competitive 4. Calibrate P1 → "Show your hand on the right!" 5. Calibrate P2 → "Show your hand on the left!" 6. Divider calculated at midpoint of wrists 7. Game begins!

Game Modes

Solo, cooperative, and competitive play

🌱

Solo Mode

1 Player

  • Full-width garden with 2–3 plant pots
  • One player interacts with all tools and plants
  • Standard challenge progression through chapters
  • DDA adjusts to single player's ability
🍋

Community Garden

Co-operative • 2 Players

  • Shared garden — both players interact with same pots
  • Shared goal: "Together, grow 8 plants!"
  • P1 = orange glove, P2 = blue glove
  • Magic Pumpkin: both touch = confetti + timer pause
  • Team celebration at round end
🏆

Village Fair

Competitive • 2 Players

  • Split garden with soft dashed divider
  • Individual scoring — each player has own zone
  • Cross-zone assist: harvest points go to zone owner
  • Rubber band: Golden Watering Can for trailing player
  • No "Game Over" — Blue Ribbon + Red Ribbon for both

The "Fair Play" DDA System

Dynamic Difficulty Adjustment — the brain of the multiplayer experience

⚖️ Invisible Equity

The AI creates a level playing field without anyone selecting a "difficulty setting." It monitors each player independently every 5 seconds and adjusts parameters to keep both in a Flow State.

📈 Metrics Tracked (Per Player)

MetricDescription
Harvest RateNumber of harvests in last 10-second window
Success RateTarget plant harvests / total harvests
Idle TimeSeconds since last hand interaction

🎮 Difficulty Parameters

ParameterRangeEffect
seedSpeed0.5x – 2.0xHow fast plant needs deplete
hitBoxMultiplier0.7x – 1.8xCollision detection radius

⚙️ Adjustment Rules

High Performer (>3 harvests/10s)🔥 Harder
Faster depletion, smaller hit boxes
Normal Player✅ Maintain
No changes
Struggling (<1 harvest/10s)🙌 Easier
30% slower depletion, 50% larger hit boxes
Idle (>5s no interaction)🤗 Magnetic Mode
Maximum ease — magnetic hit detection

All adjustments interpolate smoothly over ~2 seconds. No jarring changes. Dementia-tuned: struggling < 2 harvests/10s, idle > 3s.

Therapeutic Framework

Research-backed design for dementia care

"45% of dementia cases could be prevented or delayed"

— The Lancet Commission on Dementia, 2024 (Lancet 2024; 404: 572–628)

📚 Research-Based Principles

  • Errorless learning — no failure states, success is the default
  • Building on remaining capacities — focus on what patients CAN do
  • Multi-domain stimulation — motor + cognitive simultaneously
  • Self-efficacy through positive feedback — generous rewards
  • Social interaction — co-play for social engagement

🤖 Auto-Help System (Invisible Assistance)

When a player appears to be struggling, the game provides progressive, invisible help:

LevelTriggerResponse
115 seconds idle"Take your time! 🌱"
230 secondsTarget plant seeds get subtle glow
345 secondsSeeds move 50% slower
460 secondsOnly target plant types spawn

Design principle: Help appears naturally without drawing attention to struggle. No "Having trouble?" messages.

Accessibility

Designed for users with cognitive, motor, and visual impairments

👁 Visual

  • Minimum 32px primary text
  • WCAG AA contrast (4.5:1 min)
  • Plant names always shown with colors
  • No rapid flashing (<3 per second)
  • 70px+ hit zone targets

✋ Motor

  • Both hands tracked; either works
  • Generous hit detection windows
  • Seated play fully supported
  • 3-second dwell (no clicking)
  • DDA adapts to motor ability

🧠 Cognitive

  • Single objective always visible
  • Fixed UI positions throughout
  • No penalty for wrong touches
  • Current task always displayed
  • All challenges eventually completable
  • No time pressure
